Fast Food Restaurant Distribution and Per Capita Analysis Across the United States
Introduction Brief
Fast food restaurants are widely distributed across the United States, but their concentration varies significantly between cities and states. Understanding these distribution patterns can provide insights into regional business presence, consumer accessibility, and population-related trends. This project will analyze fast food restaurant data to identify geographic patterns and compare restaurant availability across different regions using per capita metrics.
Problem Statement
The distribution of fast food restaurants is not uniform across the United States, and it is difficult to determine which cities and states have the highest and lowest concentration of restaurants relative to their population. There is a need to analyze restaurant distribution patterns and compare regions using standardized metrics. This project aims to examine these patterns and provide insights into fast food restaurant availability across the country.
