# Fast Food Restaurant Analysis - Executive Summary

## Question 4: Top 10 Fast Food Restaurants Nationally
Based on total location counts across the country, the top chains are heavily dominated by major national brands. 
* **McDonald's** leads by a significant margin with nearly 1,900 locations in this dataset.
* **Burger King** and **Taco Bell** follow as the second and third most prevalent chains.
* Other major players in the top rankings include Wendy's, Arby's, KFC, and Domino's Pizza.

## Question 7: Fast Food Restaurant Density Per Capita
When analyzing restaurant density per 10,000 residents, the geographic distribution looks very different than pure volume counts:
* **Highest Density States:** South Dakota (SD) has the highest concentration with ~1.18 locations per 10,000 people, followed closely by Kentucky (KY), Wyoming (WY), New Mexico (NM), and Vermont (VT).
* **Lowest Density States:** New York (NY) has the lowest concentration with ~0.13 locations per 10,000 people, followed by New Jersey (NJ), California (CA), Massachusetts (MA), and Alaska (AK).
