# Fast Food Restaurant Analysis Across the United States

Project Overview

Fast food restaurants are an important part of the food industry in the United States, and their distribution varies across different cities and states. Understanding where restaurants are concentrated can provide insights into population density, consumer demand, and regional business trends.

This project analyzes fast food restaurant data across the United States to identify patterns in restaurant distribution and compare restaurant availability across cities and states using per capita metrics.

Business Questions

1. How do cities rank in terms of the most and least fast food restaurants across the United States?
2. Which cities have the most and least McDonald's locations per capita?
3. How many fast food restaurants are available per capita in each state?
4. Which fast food restaurant chains have the most locations nationally?
5. Which major cities have the most and least fast food restaurants per capita?
6. Which small cities have the most fast food restaurants per capita?
7. Which states have the most and least fast food restaurants per capita?
8. What overall trends can be observed in fast food restaurant distribution across the United States?
